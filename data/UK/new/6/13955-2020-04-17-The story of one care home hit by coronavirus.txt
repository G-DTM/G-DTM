The story of one care home hit by coronavirus
Julie Roche is a manager of a Buckinghamshire care home that usually has 45 residents. In the past few weeks she has lost 13 patients to Covid-19. She discusses the devastating impact this has had on families, staff and her remaining residents
The coronavirus has blown like a storm through Julie Roche's Buckinghamshire care home. In less than three weeks, eight of her patients with dementia died in quick succession. The total is now 13. Julie's experience reveals how the impact of Covid-19 on care home residents is far greater than official statistics so far show.
Julie tells Anushka Asthana about the devastating impact the virus has had on families, her staff and the other residents. Rob Booth, the Guardian's social affairs correspondent, discusses whether the government was adequately prepared for the impact of Covid-19 in care homes and the changes that the health secretary, Matt Hancock, is now introducing.
